Hobbies

Drawing

Drawing is a passion I acquired when I was still a kid. Watching animations like Dragon Ball, Knights of the Zodiac, Justice League, etc., I thought to myself, I want to be able to do that — and to this day, I still have the same thought hahahaha.

Reading

My love for reading started when I was a little guy who would sit beside my mom, asking her to read the Bible out loud and wanting to learn how to read myself. After I learned, I always loved the reading we did in class in subjects like Portuguese (my first language) and later English. As a child and teenager, I loved comics, especially the ones that made me question things I thought were important and whether they were really true. The first book I read without pictures was The Odyssey by Homer, and later The Dream of a Ridiculous Man by Dostoyevsky — who is now one of my favorite writers.

MMA

I discovered MMA through a friend from my previous church who practiced with another member who knew MMA and had taken classes all around the world. There, I learned the basics of stand-up and ground fighting. What I really enjoyed was Jiu-Jitsu and its submission (My favorite one was the kimura, hehe)

Sports

Childhood Sports: Futsal & Handball

As a child, even though I was chubby, I still loved playtime, especially when it involved running and jumping.

During my time in Portugal, I spent 1 year playing Futsal and 2 years playing Olympic Handball. While doing handball, before training I would play around with my friends all day—running a lot—then I would have handball training with both juniors and seniors (even though I was young, I was part of both teams because of my skills). The training sessions lasted about 2 hours, and after training, I would still run home—20 minutes of non-stop running.

In these two years of playing Handball, I participated in a few interschool championships.

Calisthenics

Before the lockdown, I didn’t do many activities after moving to Ireland, but it (the lockdown) served as a spring, making me workout at home, doing calisthenics every day. After arduous training, I was able to go from an L-sit to a handstand in one move (0.5 seconds, but still hahaha).
